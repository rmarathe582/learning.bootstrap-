Flowering plants usually face evolutionary pressure to optimise the transfer of their pollen, and this is typically reflected in the morphology of their flowers and their reproductive strategies.[81][82] Agents that transport pollen between plants are called vectors. Around 80% of flowering plants make use of biotic or living vectors. Others use abiotic or non-living vectors, or some combination of the two
